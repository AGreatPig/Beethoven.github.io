5 Most Popular Beethoven Pieces

#1: Symphony No.3 in E flat, op.55 - 'Eroica' Symphony

The essential piece that changed the musical world, Beethoven's 'Eroica' Symphony was completed in 1804. The piece was supposed to be dedicated to Napolean, until Beethoven heard Napolean had made himself Emperor. Beethoven found a new path for himself with this piece, and understood a lot of the sonata form possbilities and thematic development. This brought orchestral music to another level and marked the beginning of Beethoven's career's second period.

#2: Symphony No.9 in D minor, op.125 - 'Choral' Symphony

Symphony No.9 or the 'Chroal' Symphony is known as one of the greastest symphonies ever composed. Beethoven took the structure of a Classical Symphony to its limits. The theme of the piece is "the unity of mankind and our place in the universe", a very philosophical theme. The piece follows a conventional four-movement symphonic model, but with a little twist making it not conventional whatsoever.

#3: Moonlight Sonata, No.14 Op.27

A really famous piece, having a very slow and dark first movement. The name of the piece was inspired by moonlight that Beethoven saw while he was playing the piano. Has a dark mood, and a very likeable style to it. It sounds like it would play in the background whenever you feel sad or moody.

#4: Bagatelle in A Minor, WoO 59, "Fur Elise"

This is Beethoven's most popular composition. The song was not published during his lifetime, it was discovered 40 years after his death. This piece has an instantly recognizable melody and is known to be the best beginner's piano piece to learn. He used the same melody throughout the piece and just changed it up a bit every minute or so. It sounds really cool, and it can get stuck in your head pretty easily.

#5: Fidelio, op.72

When it came to opera, Beethoven was absolutely fantastic with it. Fidelio, his only effort in the genre, is based on a story. It tells how a person named Leonore, disguised as a prison guard named "Fidelio", rescues her husband from death. This is a story of heroism and sacrifice, which is really well shown through the piece. This was played in many theaters because of a contract that Beethoven signed to write an opera. This piece was the final result, and it is astonishing.
